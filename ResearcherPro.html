Writing Assignments & Research Tips

Written by experts

Look for sources that have been written by experts in their field. Experts usually tend to have good background knowledge and experience and learning in that topic you might be researching, and you can figure that out by going to the About page where they would usually have most of the information about the members of that blog or website.

If there is no dedicated author then focus on if the information has been published by a well-respected organisation like NY Times or Al Jazeera.

Citations

Most credible source will cite their work, which shows transparency lets readers fact-check and allows them to gain more sources to discover and research. It also indicates that the author or writer has done research and not just written what comes to their mind.

Up-to-date information

Make sure the information in the source is relevant and up to date and that it is still reliable in today's time. Using outdated information that is not going to be used can lead to inaccurate information and lead to limitations in your work. Unbiased: Try looking for sources that have a balanced view on the topic,and present various views on the topic.

Unbiased information

The source should present fair and accurate information on topics. This is something to look out for especially in topics that contain politics and other topics that have controversial opinions or conflict around it.

How To Avoid Plagiarism

CITE YOU'RE SOURCES!

When writing and stating something that has been said or taken from another website, don’t forget to state the name of the source or the authors last name. Then make sure to elaborate further on that statement you used from a different source and write about how it connects to what you were writing assignment is about. Kind of like the SEE structure evidence and explain are very differen, and you dont want your explaination to be from somewhere else. Quotation is similar to citing, it makes it clear what you were stating is not your word but rather from another source, but don't forget to state from where those exact words you have quoted are coming.

Paraphrase & Writing in you're Own Words

Paraphrasing is something we do a lot when researching, but it's important to understand that it just doesn’t mean switching up the words, but writing the information into your own words also it requires for ou to understand and explain it in a new way without changing the meaning.

Plagiarism Checkers

Don't be afraid of using plagiarism checkers, Grammarly provides a free plagiarism checker which detects what you have originally written and what has been copy pasted.
